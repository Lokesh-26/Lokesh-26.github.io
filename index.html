Lokeshwaran Manohar

Research Assistant · TU Dortmund University · Event-based Perception · 6D Pose Estimation

Research Assistant, FLW

TU Dortmund University

Dortmund, Germany

I am a Research Assistant at the Lehrstuhl für Förder- und Lagerwesen (FLW) at TU Dortmund University, where I work on event-based camera perception for high-speed object tracking in robotic grasping and logistics scenarios. I support system-level integration of mobile robots, perception modules, and real-time data pipelines in multi-robot logistics environments.

My research interests span event-based vision, 6D object pose estimation, robotic manipulation, and real-time perception for autonomous systems. I have contributed to benchmarking 6D pose estimation for mobile robots (MR6D, ICCV 2025 Workshop) and to recurrent event-based object detection for industrial multi-class recognition (MTEvent, ICRA 2026 Workshop).

I completed my M.Sc. in Automation and Robotics at TU Dortmund (2024), with a thesis on object localization and tracking for high-speed robotic grasping for mobile robots (supervised by Prof.’in Dr.-Ing. Alice Kirchheim). Prior to that, I obtained my B.Tech. in Mechatronics from the Hindustan Institute of Technology and Science, Chennai (2021), where I designed a bio-inspired quadruped robot (Valkyrie) for autonomous locomotion on uneven terrain.

selected publications

  1. ICCV
    MR6D: Benchmarking 6D Pose Estimation for Mobile Robots
    Anas Gouda, Shrutarv Awasthi, Christian Blesing, and 3 more authors
    In Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV) Workshops, Oct 2025
    Abs PDF

    We present MR6D, a benchmark for 6D pose estimation targeting mobile robot platforms in logistics and grasping scenarios. The benchmark includes diverse object categories, challenging occlusion conditions, and evaluation on state-of-the-art methods including FoundationPose.

  2. ICRA
    Benchmarking Recurrent Event-Based Object Detection for Industrial Multi-Class Recognition on MTEvent
    Lokeshwaran Manohar and Moritz Roidl
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2603.21787, 2026
    Abs

    We introduce MTEvent, an industrial multi-class event camera dataset, and benchmark recurrent event-based object detection models for real-time industrial recognition, demonstrating the advantages of event cameras for high-speed, low-latency perception in logistics environments.

  3. Appl. Sci.
    Valkyrie—Design and development of gaits for quadruped robot using particle swarm optimization
    Taarun Srinivas, Adithya Krishna Karigiri Madhusudhan, Lokeshwaran Manohar, and 4 more authors
    Applied Sciences, 2021
    Abs DOI

    We present the design and development of Valkyrie, a bio-inspired quadruped robot, and propose a particle swarm optimization approach for generating stable locomotion gaits. The robot is capable of navigating uneven terrain, demonstrated through simulation and physical prototype evaluation.
