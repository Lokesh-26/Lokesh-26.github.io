I am a Research Assistant at the [Lehrstuhl für Förder- und Lagerwesen (FLW)](https://flw.mb.tu-dortmund.de/){:target="_blank"} at TU Dortmund University, where I work on **event-based camera perception** for high-speed object tracking in robotic grasping and logistics scenarios. I support system-level integration of mobile robots, perception modules, and real-time data pipelines in multi-robot logistics environments.

My research interests span **event-based vision**, **6D object pose estimation**, **robotic manipulation**, and **real-time perception** for autonomous systems. I have contributed to benchmarking 6D pose estimation for mobile robots ([MR6D, ICCV 2025 Workshop](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/ICCV2025W/R6D/papers/Gouda_MR6D_Benchmarking_6D_Pose_Estimation_for_Mobile_Robots_ICCVW_2025_paper.pdf)) and to recurrent event-based object detection for industrial multi-class recognition ([MTEvent, ICRA 2026 Workshop](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21787)).

I completed my **M.Sc. in Automation and Robotics** at TU Dortmund (2024), with a thesis on object localization and tracking for high-speed robotic grasping for mobile robots (supervised by Prof.'in Dr.-Ing. Alice Kirchheim). Prior to that, I obtained my **B.Tech. in Mechatronics** from the Hindustan Institute of Technology and Science, Chennai (2021), where I designed a bio-inspired quadruped robot ([Valkyrie](https://doi.org/10.3390/app11167458)) for autonomous locomotion on uneven terrain.
