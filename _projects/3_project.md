This project enhanced the **autonomous walking capabilities** of the NAO humanoid robot by applying modern **reinforcement learning** techniques to upper body control, targeting improved locomotion performance for humanoid robotic soccer applications.

**Key contributions:**

- Implemented a **Twin-Delayed Deep Deterministic policy gradient (TD3)** agent to learn continuous upper body joint control policies.
- Enhanced the NAO robot's ability to maintain balance and perform **continuous walking actions** with improved stability across diverse terrain conditions.
- Evaluated the learned policy in simulation and analyzed trade-offs between learning speed, locomotion smoothness, and task performance.
